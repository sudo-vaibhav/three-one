📜 Preface

All the cloud providers conveniently try to tell you in their documentation and marketing material that self-hosting is a bad idea. Cloud is often expressed as the cheap and perfect solution to all hosting problems. But it's not.

2021 was probably the worst year ever for cloud uptime SLAs and in most cases, you can buy a hardware equivalent of your cloud VM and host it yourself, and within a few months, you would have recovered your initial capital investment, when compared to the expensive cloud pricing.

What I have said above is valid for most small businesses and folks who are just starting out. Your apps are not going to be flooded by millions of users anytime soon. And no matter how much you'd like to think so, your app doesn't require a 24x7 uptime.

So I made this website and decided to self-host it from an old RPi I had lying around.

🌦️ Cloudflare to the Rescue

Cloudflare Tunnels create an encrypted tunnel between your server and the Cloudflare network using the cloudflared daemon. Because of this, users won't directly connect to your server, instead they will connect via proxy through Cloudflare's network.

🏆 Benefits

  • There is no need to open any ports on your network. To the outside world and even your ISP, your server just looks like any other encrypted web traffic.
  • There is no need to create, buy or mange TLS certificates as cloudflared handles all of this for you.
  • Cloudflare utilizes smart routing to speed up traffic latency. They avoid congested network and high latency area which routing the traffic through their edge network.
  • You get a WAF and DDOS protection built in.
